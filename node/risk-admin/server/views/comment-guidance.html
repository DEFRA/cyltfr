We recommend that you use the Chrome browser for uploading holding comments.

The holding comment functionality should:

  • Not include any personal information
  • Include a shapefile that covers a specific geographic area
  • Provide additional information not yet included in the mapping
  • Not include any hyperlinks within the text - they will not work
  • Be temporary - and removed when they are no longer valid

The information you provide is yours to own via your normal custodianship for local flood information.

Shapefile guidance

  • Your shapefile must be zipped. You can have multiple polygons within the same shapefile.
  • Shapefiles will not be displayed in map view - they are only used to capture the area in which to display the information.
  • Your comment will only appear when somebody searches for an address that falls within your shapefile extent.
  • Your shapefile should cover both the area impacted directly by the information as well as any receptors affected by the change to the area.
  • Attributes will be auto populated if they match directly with those on the service. If they do not you can manually fill them in.

Guidance for writing content on GOV.UK

  • Use plain English. Read words to avoid
  • Write short clear sentences
  • You can use technical terms but you must explain them
  • Avoid abbreviations and acronyms
  • Do not write blocks of text in capitals
  • Write dates like this: 4 June 2017
  • Use ‘to’ in date ranges rather than -
  • Avoid negative contractions like don’t - use do not
  • Avoid ampersands, use ‘and’
  • Numbers: generally use numerals but there are exceptions

For further guidance read:

  • Environment Agency Data Protection
  • Writing for GOV.UK
  • Government Digital Service style guide
