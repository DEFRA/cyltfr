How we describe risk

This is how we describe the risk of flooding from surface water, rivers and the sea.

Risk Level Likelihood
High Greater than 1 in 30
Medium Between 1 in 30 and 1 in 100
Low Between 1 in 100 and 1 in 1000
Very Low Less than 1 in 1000

These figures may seem low, but an area categorised as high risk is located in one of the areas most likely to flood in England.

  • 5.9 million properties are at risk of flooding - 1 in 6 homes
  • £28,000 - the average cost of a home flooding

A property can still flood if it’s:

  • on a hill
  • not near a body of water like a river or stream
Read more about who is at risk of flooding and the impact

Anybody can be affected by flooding - even people who live on top floor flats. It can affect people in ways that might not seem obvious at first.

It can impact your:
  • health - through disease or impact on mental health
  • access to clean water
  • gas and electricity supplies
  • drainage - sewage has nowhere to go
  • doctor’s surgery or hospital
  • school or place of work
  • community centre or place of worship
  • shops or supermarkets
